About TPMS Service at Bingo Tire & Auto Service

Professional TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring System) diagnosis, reset, and sensor replacement at all five Bingo Tire & Auto Service locations in Northern Virginia. Don't ignore your tire pressure warning light — proper tire pressure is critical for safety, fuel efficiency, and tire longevity. We service TPMS systems on all makes and models in Springfield, Woodbridge, Alexandria, Centreville, and Winchester.

The TPMS warning light on your dashboard is required by federal law on all vehicles manufactured since 2008 — it's there to alert you when one or more tires drop below a safe pressure threshold. But the TPMS system itself requires maintenance that many drivers overlook. Sensors use an internal battery that typically lasts 5 to 10 years. When a sensor battery dies, the TPMS light comes on and stays on — even if all your tires are perfectly inflated. At Bingo Tire & Auto Service, we use specialized TPMS scan tools to identify exactly which sensor has failed and whether the issue is the sensor, the battery, the valve stem, or the system's programming.

A common cause of TPMS issues in Northern Virginia is the seasonal temperature swing. As temperatures drop in fall and winter, tire pressure decreases — roughly 1 PSI for every 10°F drop in temperature. This can push borderline tires below the sensor threshold, triggering the warning light even on tires that were properly inflated in warmer weather. Adding 2 to 3 PSI in the fall is good preventive practice. If your light comes on and all four tires are at the correct pressure, a failed sensor is likely the cause.
